Anand Sankar

Day 0

The first thing I heard when I arrived to the Venue at Kalasalingam University the day before the start of the workshop was ‘3D printer’ and I more or less internally shrieked, because I realized then that the following week was going to be amazing.

Day 1

I woke up way earlier than I was supposed to, and spent most of my time trying to figure out how to take a bath without a bucket. But that got solved sooner than I thought, and I made it to the venue early.

Day 2

Day two started with me oversleeping and almost being late for the session that morning, but luckily, I managed to shake off the sleep.

Day 3

The day started with a small session where we had to interview three people from different teams, and also get interviewed by three people. I got caught up in all the fun and ended up interviewing six people and got interviewed by four.

Day 4

Day four started with us getting our very own TechTop T-shirts as we prepared to go for another field trip.

Day 5

Day Five started out really fast. We began to finalize on a problem, and make a product to tackle the problem and find a solution. My team settled on safety issues for the workers in the spinning mill, because of my personal experience with the cotton fibre getting in my eye as well as the lack of masks and safety equipment worn by the workers, plus their feedback on why they weren’t using them.

Day 6

Day Six was a direct continuation of Day Five, zero sleep but the adrenaline kept me going, at about 05:00 AM, Anand Parikh and some of the mentors asked me and the rest of the guys who were still at the lab if we wanted to go trekking to the nearby mountain (actually it was a really big and steep hill but mountain seems apt and sounds cooler) and we rounded up some guys and called some other people who were sleeping like Madhav, another mentor and Abhishek(from Team F), and we all went trekking.

Day 7

I ended up staying over at the lab till about 04:30 AM and while we were going back, the wind was intense, as in it almost felt like a hurricane, but we later heard from some people that monstrous wind was normal in the area. But it was still unnerving, as I went back with Anuvind, near the hostel there was something a bit off, and as we got closer, we noticed that the wind was so intense that the glass door broke off the hinges and was lying in pieces.
