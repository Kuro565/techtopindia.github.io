Day-4

As per yesterday's instruction we all were ready to go for second field trip,so we boarded the college bus at 9:30am by wearing Techtop T-shirt. The journey was really nice,we sang,danced,and talked with everyone.It was really a cool experience.

 After 30 min of journey we reached our first spot,the Arumuga Spinning mill.

This industry actually,produce a large amount of cotton and it covers approx 75% of India. The mill was really huge in size and the diff process are done in different sections,so we had to get on to one after another.

Photography was prohibited so couldn't click any.

The first section was were they had a bunch of fresh cottons collected on a big chamber like thing and with the help of suction it's been sucked and passed on to diff operation through a carrier tunnels,the cottons were separated like good and bad.The good ones were sent on to the next section.

And by passing on and on the cotton has become more thinner, lighter,softer and more refined.

The catchy thing for me was that systematic arrangement of cotton bundle and the pattern in which those cotton layers are placed.

From this,thick bundle again through other operations it's made more thinner and thinner,and with all these thinner cotton threads they are rolled on a plastic rod. The process was really stunning me,the technology have developed to a level were less manpower is required. And it all worked more organised manner.

The place was well neat and tidy with all time supervisor walking up and down.The workers seem happier in their job and doing it sincerely.

With that we left the milling industry with a  selfie from Rajesh sir.

The next spot is really watering my mouth even while thinking of it now.

A small milk product manufacture called Sri Venkiteswara shop,where they make their own products at the backyard and sell it in shops.

 So this is how it actually looks.

They make product called Palguva with 20 litres of milk mixed with 2-3 kg of sugar and heat it under 80-85°C.

After,1hr of stirring both manually and mechanically it gradually started to harden and the outcome is just 6 kg of Palguva.

 The room was really hot and unbearable heat which we couldn't stay there for more than half an hour.So we rushed back to get on to bus.

So before leaving the room,the operator came to us  with tumbler of Palguva product and offered us to taste it.

Oooh maan the taste was heavenly and I really want to grab more so I bought 2 packets of those so that I can have it later or  if possibly I can bring one packet to my home.

Got on to our bus,reached back the college hostel and went for another visit to visually impaired graduates who study in the same college.

Now,this was different we had chat with grown-up people who are graduates but visually impaired either by birth or later life. Somewhere partially,fully blind and also people with low vision,short vision etc.

The students in this class had a computer and a headphone with them.They have learnt lots of shortcuts in keyboards and they knew more than any normal person.

So with the help of microphones they are assisted with voice.

Such a way that whatever they press or select,will be read through the microphones and needn't have their monitor to screen on.

I think it would be more helpful if the voice assistant is done in the respective native language of the user.

After we left him by saying bye,he resumed his work from where it was stopped.
